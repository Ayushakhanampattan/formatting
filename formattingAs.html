Regional language

A regional language is a language primarily spoken in a specific geographic area or region ,rather than being a national de facto language. The specific regional languages vary by country, but examples from India, which recognizes a number of official regional languages, include Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Punjabi, Tamil, and Telugu.

National language

India has no single national language; the Constitution designates Hindi as the official language of the Union, with English serving as an associate official language. The country's rich linguistic diversity is recognized through the Eighth Schedule, which lists 22 official languages that states can choose for their own use.
Hindi:As per Article 343 of the Indian Constitution, Hindi is the primary language for communication by the Central Governmentwith Hindi-speaking states.

Universal language

There isn't a single, fully recognized "universal language" that everyone on Earth speaks; however, English is widely considered the closest to a universal language due to its extensive use in international business, science, media, and the internet, effectively serving as a global lingua franca. Other concepts that are sometimes called universal languages include mathematical expressions and music, which can transcend linguistic barriers, though not in the same way a spoken language does
